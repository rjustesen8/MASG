Why this matters

Athletics are too important to run on guesswork alone.

Athletic departments influence student experience, school culture, and community connection, but many schools still face rising costs, limited budgets, and year-to-year decision-making without strong planning tools. MASG exists to help schools organize that complexity into clearer choices.

What schools receive

Clearer visibility into what athletics costs, where support is coming from, and what deserves closer review.

Clearer financial visibility

Understand where athletic dollars are going, which cost buckets create the most pressure, and where hidden inefficiencies may be sitting.

Program-level decision support

See athletics by sport, by resource use, and by planning need instead of as one broad line item inside a larger school budget.

Board-ready reporting

Translate complex athletics questions into clean summaries, practical recommendations, and stronger communication with school leadership.

A planning framework

Move from reacting year to year toward a more disciplined view of travel, equipment, outside support, and long-term program sustainability.

How it works

One focused process from school context to a clearer next step.

MASG is not trying to overcomplicate the first engagement. The process is built to gather the context, organize the information, and turn it into something a school can actually use.

01

Gather the athletics picture

Collect the available budget details, rosters, travel patterns, support sources, and operating context that shape how athletics actually functions.

02

Organize the information

Standardize data into useful categories so leaders can compare spending, support, and program-level pressure more clearly.

03

Translate it into decisions

Build visuals, summaries, and findings that make the information usable for real school conversations rather than internal guesswork.

04

Recommend the next move

Turn findings into a practical path forward, whether that is a snapshot review, a broader audit, or a more strategic planning effort.

Frequently asked questions

Answer the practical questions without overstating the business.

What does MASG actually do?

MASG helps public high schools understand athletics spending, athletics support, and program-level pressure so leaders can make more disciplined decisions and plan with greater clarity.

Who is this built for?

The site is built for athletic directors, principals, superintendents, district leaders, and other school decision-makers who need better visibility into how athletics is functioning.

Do you need current client proof to inquire?

No. MASG is early stage, so the site uses mock examples and clear service framing rather than pretending to have public case studies that do not exist yet.

What is the best first step?

For most schools, the best first step is a smaller Athletic Program Snapshot so the school can start with a lower-risk review and decide whether a larger engagement makes sense.
