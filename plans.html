How MASG works

A repeatable process.

The process moves from raw school information to a usable strategy guide with priorities, funding ideas, fundraising guidance, support-building options, and next-season actions.

01

Organize the data

Collect budgets, rosters, schedules, travel, facilities, fundraising, and equipment-cycle information.

02

Find pressure points

Review cost per athlete, sport spending, travel exposure, revenue gaps, and replacement risks.

03

Build the guide

Turn findings into priorities, funding opportunities, fundraising guidance, and a clear implementation direction that matches the school's scope.

04

Plan next season

Leave the department with a structure it can update, explain, and use again.

What MASG uses

Built from practical department data.

MASG can begin with public information and sharpen the strategy with internal records.

Sport budgets

Spending by team, category, and season.

Participation rosters

Athletes by sport, grade, gender, and retention.

Travel data

Contest locations, mileage, bus use, and outlier trips.

Equipment cycles

Uniform ages, safety gear, facility needs, and replacement timing.

Event revenue

Gate, concessions, livestream, sponsors, camps, and fundraising activity.

Booster activity

Fundraising calendars, project asks, sponsor renewals, and community or local-government backing efforts.

FAQ

Questions before requesting a plan.

What is a Strategy Guide?

A focused review that identifies planning gaps, funding opportunities, fundraising paths, and priority actions.

How is pricing determined?

Pricing depends on plan level, school size, number of sports, data readiness, funding support needs, timeline, and overall school need.

What data do you need?

Budgets, rosters, schedules, travel, equipment cycles, revenue, sponsors, and booster activity all help.

Can MASG help with fundraising support?

Yes. Where needed, MASG can include guides for sponsor outreach, different fundraising options, and ways to build support around funding plans with community and local-government stakeholders.

Is the sample official?

No. The sample report is a public-data mock deliverable that shows the style and structure.

What happens after the form?

The next step is a short discovery conversation to clarify goals, data, timeline, funding support needs, and scope.
