Mock paper
Program Review Summary Illustrative example for a Midwest public high school

Primary findings

  • Travel and operations create more pressure than leaders expected.
  • Outside support is meaningful but tracked unevenly across programs.
  • Equipment planning is more reactive than strategic.
  • Leaders need a cleaner sport-by-sport decision framework.

Recommended next step

Build a one-year planning view that aligns travel, equipment, and outside support questions with the school’s larger athletics priorities.

Illustrative planning table

Show the shift from current state to recommended direction.

Even a mock example can help a school picture how cleaner athletics reporting improves conversations around reallocation, planning, and communication.

Metric Current Recommended Strategic impact
Travel planning Built mainly around season-to-season habit Reviewed against scheduling and cost patterns Better visibility into avoidable travel strain
Equipment planning Reactive and uneven by sport Mapped into a more deliberate replacement view Stronger long-term planning conversations
Outside support tracking Varies by program and source Organized into one clearer summary structure Improved reporting and easier leadership communication
Program-level visibility Broad athletics view only Sport-by-sport decision framing Better prioritization and more disciplined tradeoff discussions
What this kind of review can clarify
  • Cost pressure by sport or by athletics function
  • Travel and contest-related strain
  • Outside support dependence and visibility gaps
  • Equipment and facility planning pressure
  • Questions that deserve a larger strategic review
Why this page matters right now

Without public case studies yet, MASG can still show the structure of the work. That helps schools understand what they could receive, how the information would be organized, and what kinds of decisions the process is built to support.

The point is not to overclaim traction. The point is to make the business look honest, disciplined, and usable now.
