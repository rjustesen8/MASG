About

Strategy first. Athletic specific. Midwest focused.

Midwest Athletic Strategy Group exists to help schools make better athletics decisions through stronger planning, clearer visibility, and a more disciplined way to think about program-level pressure.

What MASG is built around

Athletic departments deserve more than one-year guesswork.

MASG is built around the idea that schools need stronger tools for understanding how athletics uses resources, where support is uneven, where inefficiencies may exist, and how a clearer strategy framework can strengthen program sustainability.

The point is not just cutting costs. The point is helping schools make smarter decisions, protect student opportunity, and better match the significance of athletics with a more organized planning structure.

Founder perspective

Reid Justesen is the founder of Midwest Athletic Strategy Group. He is a Kansas State strategic management graduate with minors in entrepreneurship and leadership, plus certificates in sales and international business.

That background shapes the MASG perspective: athletic departments benefit when leaders can turn complexity into clearer decisions, stronger communication, and more disciplined planning. Reid cares about this work because athletics carry real meaning inside schools and communities, yet many programs still operate without planning structure that matches that importance.

Mission

Help schools turn athletic information into clearer strategy.

MASG helps athletic directors, administrators, and school leaders build more sustainable athletics decisions by organizing what matters, clarifying pressure points, and supporting more deliberate next steps.

Brand direction

Founder-led without making the site entirely founder-centric.

The website should feel bigger than one biography. The founder section is here to provide honest context, while the center of gravity stays on the school problem, the service model, and the outcomes a school is trying to improve.
